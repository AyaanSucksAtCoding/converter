The History of Kilometers and Miles

The kilometer is a unit of length in the metric system, equivalent to one thousand meters. It was introduced during the French Revolution when the metric system was first established in 1799. The mile, on the other hand, is a unit of length used in the imperial system, commonly used in the United States and the United Kingdom. It originates from the Roman mille passus, which was equivalent to 5,000 Roman feet.
